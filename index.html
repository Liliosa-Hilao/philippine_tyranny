Liliosa Hilao: A Martyr of Martial Law

Liliosa Rapi Hilao (March 14, 1950 – April 5, 1973) was a Filipina student journalist and activist who became a symbol of resistance against the oppressive rule of Martial Law in the Philippines. She is remembered as the first political detainee to die in detention under Martial Law.

Early Life and Activism

A talented and dedicated student, Liliosa excelled academically, consistently earning honors throughout her schooling. She was a Communication Arts student at the Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila (PLM), where she also served as an editor for the student paper, *Hasik*. While she didn't participate in street protests due to health issues, her strong convictions and sharp intellect found expression in her insightful and critical writings. Her articles, such as "The Vietnamization of the Philippines" and "Democracy is Dead in the Philippines under Martial Law," challenged the Marcos regime and its policies.

“Democracy is dead.” - Liliosa Hilao, in her diary, upon the declaration of Martial Law.

Arrest and Death

In April 1973, Liliosa's life was tragically cut short. She was arrested by soldiers from the Constabulary Anti-Narcotics Unit who raided her family's home. Witnesses reported that she was subjected to brutal treatment, including torture and sexual abuse, while in custody at Camp Crame. The official narrative claimed she committed suicide, but this was widely disputed. The circumstances surrounding her death strongly suggest she was a victim of state-sponsored violence.

Legacy

Liliosa Hilao's death became a rallying cry against the abuses of Martial Law. Her sacrifice inspired many to fight for justice and democracy. She is remembered as a symbol of courage, conviction, and resistance. Her name is inscribed on the Bantayog ng mga Bayani's Wall of Remembrance, honoring the martyrs and heroes of Martial Law. A street in her hometown of Bulan, Sorsogon, also bears her name.

Liliosa Hilao's story serves as a reminder of the importance of vigilance in safeguarding human rights and democratic freedoms. Her legacy continues to inspire generations of Filipinos to stand up against injustice.

Key Details

  • Full Name: Liliosa Rapi Hilao
  • Born: March 14, 1950, Bulan, Sorsogon, Philippines
  • Died: April 5, 1973, Quezon City, Philippines
  • Alma Mater: Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila
  • Known for: First prisoner to die in detention during Martial Law
